Process Overview

Pyrolysis is a thermochemical decomposition of organic material at elevated temperatures in the absence of oxygen. It produces three main products: biochar (solid), bio-oil (liquid), and syngas (gas).

The process occurs in several stages: drying, pyrolysis proper, and cooling. Each stage requires precise temperature control to optimize product yields.

Key Advantages

  • Converts waste biomass into valuable products
  • Reduces greenhouse gas emissions compared to incineration
  • Produces renewable energy sources
  • Creates carbon-negative biochar for soil amendment
  • Reduces waste volume by up to 90%

Applications

  • Municipal solid waste treatment
  • Agricultural residue processing
  • Plastic waste conversion
  • Biomass energy production
  • Soil improvement with biochar

Pyrolysis Process Flow

①
Feedstock Input

Biomass is loaded into the chamber through the inlet system

②
Oxygen Removal

Chamber is purged with inert gas to create oxygen-free environment

③
Heating Phase

Temperature is gradually increased to pyrolysis range

④
Decomposition

Biomass breaks down into biochar, bio-oil, and syngas

⑤
Product Collection

Products are separated, collected, and stored for further use
